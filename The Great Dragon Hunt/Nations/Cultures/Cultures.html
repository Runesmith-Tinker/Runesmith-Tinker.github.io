The Cultures of Fluia

A brief overview

Below is each of the cultures that have grown into existence across the Continents of Fluia. On this shattered fragment of a long dead world, there were only four tribes in the beginning that survived The Schism. While there were some survivors that were not of the four clans, almost all modern ideologies and peoples descend from those mighty tribes.

Claiming the frozen wastes to the north west were the giants.
Hoarding mountains and open vistas to the south west were the dragons.
Cultivating the marshes and woodlands of the south east were the elves.
Carving out the mountains and depths of the north east were the dwarves.

However, those days have long since passed, with each culture spreading and shaping one another across the world. Barely anything of the founding tribes still exist. At least to the prying eyes of the "civilized folk." For the giants live all but in seclusion to the far north beyond where it is habitable for most. Dragons having spread themselves wide have either turned to acting like monsters, or chosen to integrate with modern civilization and even take on more humanoid forms. The Trow live in sparse ever moving groves, feeling shunned by their descendants for sticking to the old ways. The dwarves of the Dunirr have opted mostly to simply secede from the surface, spreading out across the vast cave networks and chasms of the underdark beneath Fluia.
Nowadays, mostly the descendants of those ancient cultures intermingle above ground. Their nations have spread across almost every corner of the lands, as shown below.

As a short forward to the below, please bear in mind that what is written is built around the core beliefs and customs of each culture. Each is described with the practices and beliefs of a purist of each culture to express the deepest views that might be held and expressed by those peoples. The majority of people are not pure idealists of any one given culture, but instead usually take some core beliefs from their own culture and will through life mix in the views of others they encounter.
