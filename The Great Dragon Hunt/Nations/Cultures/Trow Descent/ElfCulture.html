Elven Culture

The Trow

Also known as Drow, as miss-spelled by many, or referred to as "dark elves" in a somewhat deragatory fashion by most non-Trow elves.

Trow Gods

Chief Deity:
Aellra, the Goddess of the Sea, Nature, and the Stars. She is depicted as very plain, with tanned skin, waist length flowing dark golden hair, and eyes the color of the night sky. She is very tall and muscular, and generally has a blissful expression. She usually wears minimal clothing that is generally somewhat transparent and silver.
She is also associated with marriage and medicine with many of the traditions to honor her being preformed late at night by the sea side. Her many followers can be primarily distinguished by their hairstyle. There are almost always a small amount of offerings at any of her shrines. Among her people there are many myths involving her competetive relationship with Ereao. She is the mother of Adackenath. She is known to give help to charismatic mortals.

Other Important Deities:
Ereao, the God of Summer, Dreams, and Mountains. He is depicted as unusually beautiful, with bronze skin, shoulder length dark brown hair and and golden eyes like two burning suns in the heat of summer. He is medium height and muscular, and often looks sly. He usually wears scant clothing, dyed dark bronze and bright green.
He is also associated with beauty and destruction with many of his followers tending to be women. His almost fanatical followers can be primarily distinguished by the magic they wield. There are more than a few altars dedicated to him on islands. There are many myths involving his ill-fated romance with Shetath. He is the brother of Ilazerlo. He is known to challenge virtuous mortals.

Raeoean, the Goddess of Wind, and Earth, is somewhat plain, with ebony skin, long, straight light brown hair, and eyes the color of banked coals. She is very tall and very muscular, and almost never smiles. She usually wears formal attire dyed dark green and beige.
She is also associated with arrogance and birds. She is often worshipped by commoners. Her few, but dedicated followers can be primarily distinguished by their jewelry. There are a large number of sacrifices to her in the foothills of mountains. There are many myths involving her competetive relationship with certain mortals. She is the daughter of El'ryi. She is known to give help to mortal warriors.

Shetath, the God of Fire, is very beautiful, with sallow skin, shoulder length, curly black hair, and eyes the color of moonlight. He is short and of hearty build, and is usually frowning. He usually wears scant clothing, dyed dark aqua.
He is also associated with cities. He is often worshipped by the poor. His surprisingly numerous followers can be primarily distinguished by their ritual scars. There are widespread temples to him at crossroads. There are many myths involving his affectionate relationship with Tian'dur. He is the brother of Adackenath. He is known to send dreams to peasants who ask for aid.

El'ryi, the God of Thunder, and Nature, is extremely beautiful, with very pale skin, long, pale red hair, and eyes the color of fresh snow. He is tall and slender, and generally has a blissful expression. He usually wears a tattered cloak dyed yellow-green.
He is also associated with fog, good and orphans. He is often worshipped by the wealthy. His numerous followers can be primarily distinguished by their hairstyle. There are widespread offerings to him on islands. There are many myths involving his rough relationship with Adackenath. He is the brother of Adackenath. He is known to give advice to mortals seeking forgiveness.

Ilazerlo, the God of Brawls, and Death, is very plain, with bronzed skin, waist length, flowing dark brown hair, and eyes the color of jade. He is short and solidly built, and sometimes seems very sad. He usually wears chain mail dyed medium red.
He is also associated with thieves. He is often worshipped by the wealthy. His many followers can be primarily distinguished by the ritual greetings they use. There are more than a few carvings of him on lonely roads. There are many myths involving his duels with Raeoean. He is the cousin of Tian'dur. He is known to send dreams to mortal leaders.

Inaesku, the God of Archery, Dawn, and Magic. He is depicted as on the mature side, being very plain in appearance, with very pale skin, shoulder length curly white hair, and eyes the color of gleaming steel. He is tall and somewhat muscular, and has a faraway expression. He usually wears a hooded cloak dyed grey-blue and pale violet.
He is also associated with mercenaries and famine, with criminals being among his most common followers. His many followers can be primarily distinguished by the clothing they wear. There are a large number of shrines to him in the foothills of mountains. There are many myths involving his tempestuous relationship with Aellra and one suggesting it might have been one of his followers that cursed Tian'dur. He is the brother of Shetath. He is known to send dreams to mortal warriors in warning of impending danger.

Adackenath, the Goddess of the Sky. She is depicted as young and very plain in appearance, with ashen skin, waist length, wavy red hair that fades from deep red to pale yellow like a sunset, and eyes that shift from blue like a clear sky to dark grey like a cloudy overcast depending on her mood. She is very tall and broad-shouldered, and tends to look rather smug. She usually wears a hooded cloak dyed pale blue over top a deep blue dress with a skirt reaching her knees.
She is also associated with sleep, passage to other realms, and traveling. She is often worshipped by adventurers. Her few followers can be primarily distinguished by the ritual greetings they use. There are numerous shrines to her at crossroads. There are many myths involving her adventures with El'ryi. She is the daughter of Shetath. She is known to appear to mortals in dire need to offer them guidance.

Tian'dur, the Goddess of Wishes, Witches, and winter. She is depicted as young with having an almost ethereal beauty to her, with stark white skin, short pale blue hair, and eyes the color of glacial ice. She is very short with wiry build, and seldom smiles. She usually wears white flowing robes that appear coated in frost and jewelery made of silver and crystals that look like ice. However, she has also been known to take the form of a small white snow bird as an avatar.
She is also associated with fate, justice and creation. She is often worshipped by a select few mages that she has bestowed a blessing upon. Her few, but dedicated followers can be primarily distinguished by the magic they wield. There are widespread sacrifices to her in small villages. There are many myths involving her many misadventures with Adackenath, including one where a curse was bestowed upon her that aimed to cause her frost to kill her followers. She is the daughter of Adackenath. She is known to give some help to great mortal warriors.

The Eldar

Eldar Gods

Chief Deities:
Oroah, the Goddess of the Plains, Peace and Celebration, is blindingly beautiful, with fair skin, waist length, straight pale red hair, and eyes the color of stormy seas. She is medium height and slender, and usually has a guarded expression. She usually wears a hooded cloak dyed tan and dark blue.
She is also associated with witches. She is often worshipped by men. Her many followers can be primarily distinguished by their tattoos. There are a few depictions of her in most cities. There are many myths involving her rough relationship with Z'skah. She is the cousin of Drousher. She is known to help mortals in dire need.

Enat, the Goddess of Innocence and Good, is blindingly beautiful, with bronzed skin, long, wavy black hair, and eyes the color of green leaves. She is short and slender, and almost never frowns. She usually wears little clothing, dyed pale violet and pale blue.
She is also associated with winter and orphans. She is often worshipped by merchants. Her numerous followers can be primarily distinguished by their secret language. There are a few carvings of her just outside of cities. There are many myths involving her epic battles with Drousher. She is the daughter of Gukol. She is known to help great mortal leaders.

Other Important Deities:
Drousher, the God of Medicine, is almost plain-looking, with tanned skin, long, curly red-brown hair, and eyes the color of cold iron. He is short and very slender, and looks rather smug. He usually wears a cloak dyed dark bronze and gold.
He is also associated with birds and crossroads. He is often worshipped by men. His many followers can be primarily distinguished by their ritual scars. There are widespread altars dedicated to him in small villages. There are many myths involving his unhappy relationship with Chouaxa. He is the husband of Enat. He is known to give aid to mortal magic-users.

Gukol, the Goddess of Dogs, Rivers and Astrology, is extremely beautiful, with very pale skin, long, cinnamon hair, and eyes the color of dark earth. She is medium height and slender, and almost never frowns. She usually wears elegant clothing dyed dark green.
She is also associated with ghosts and graves. She is often worshipped by nobles. Her many followers can be primarily distinguished by the clothing they wear. There are a respectable amount of temples to her in most cities. There are many myths involving her friendship with Enat. She is the daughter of Gyyl. She is known to give aid to distressed mortals.

Z'skah, the Goddess of Deceit and Summer, is an exotic beauty, with tanned skin, short, flowing black hair, and eyes the color of fire opals. She is tall and somewhat muscular, and tends to look sad. She usually wears a hooded cloak dyed beige and pale brown.
She is also associated with vengeance. She is often worshipped by magic-users. Her many followers can be primarily distinguished by the ritual greetings they use. There are a respectable amount of offerings to her in meadows. There are many myths involving her ill-fated romance with Dir. She is the daughter of Gukol. She is known to give aid to mortal heroes.

Kalyus, the God of Desire, the Moon and Darkness, is surprisingly plain, with brown skin, short, swirling gold hair, and eyes the color of the night sky. He is tall and slightly plump, and looks somewhat malicious. He usually wears armor dyed light grey.
He is also associated with evil. He is often worshipped by commoners. His vast flocks of followers can be primarily distinguished by the ritual greetings they use. There are quite a few temples to him in forests. There are many myths involving his lustful relationship with Dir. He is the son of Dir. He is known to give some help to mortal heroes.

Dir, the Goddess of Whistling and Opposites, is blindingly beautiful, with copper skin, short, swirling auburn hair, and eyes the color of the blue ocean. She is tall and slightly plump, and looks rather smug. She usually wears leather armor dyed dark aqua.
She is also associated with poetry, famine and intoxicants. She is often worshipped by village folk. Her many followers can be primarily distinguished by the clothing they wear. There are many depictions of her just outside of cities. There are many myths involving her illicit relationship with Gukol. She is the sister of Chouaxa. She is known to send visions to mortal heroes.

Chouaxa, the Goddess of Mercenaries, is unusually beautiful, with dark skin, short, flowing pale red hair, and eyes the color of sapphires. She is tall and of hearty build, and tends to look sad. She usually wears armor and a helmet dyed black.
She is also associated with thunder, tricksters and psychology. She is often worshipped by men. Her almost fanatical followers can be primarily distinguished by the clothing they wear. There are widespread statues of her at the edge of forests. There are many myths involving her illicit relationship with Gyyl. She is the wife of Enat. She is known to send visions to mortals in dire need.

Gyyl, the God of the Undead and Passion, is extremely beautiful, with tanned skin, long, wavy flaming red hair, and eyes the color of new-forged copper. He is very tall and very slender, and generally has a blissful expression. He usually wears chain mail dyed grey-brown and dark brown.
He is also associated with joy, cities and messengers. He is often worshipped by city dwellers. His many followers can be primarily distinguished by the way they conduct themselves. There are many temples to him in military outposts. There are many myths involving his unhealthy relationship with Kalyus. He is the husband of Oroah. He is known to send visions to mortal heroes.
