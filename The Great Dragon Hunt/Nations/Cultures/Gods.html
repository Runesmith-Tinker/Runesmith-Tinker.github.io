Gods

In the lands of Fluia, there are only a handful of true dieties. While there do exist spirits, lesser "gods", or demigods of the lands and of different tribes, villages, and even some aspects of the world that are offered homage and prayed to, they do not rise to the level of true godhood. Of the true gods that do exist, having seemingly risen to their grandure out of the void that is the Dark Era, there are six. However, each of the six is known to express themselves in many different ways, as their own aspects shift across the different natures of what they uphold to reign over different domains. Thus while two cultures may be beholden to the same prime deity, the aspect of that deity they may revere will most likely be different and as such depicted and worshipped vastly differently. As such, while no one god belongs to a singular people, each god is more frequently depicted with aspects of one ancestry and gender than of any other.

Zenetta

The sun goddess, also known as the lustful dragon, is one of the most promonant gods across all of Fluia. Despite her name, she is not actually associated to the domain of passion. Instead her monicure comes from her eternally chasing after the tempermental trickster moon goddess. Yet even through her desire to be with the one that she loves, she still takes the time to watch over the world and give light to those needing it for work. As such, most laborers and dayworkers will often wear pendants marked with either her Zenith of Setting symbol. It is also not uncommon for adventurers to addorn their armour with symbols of her Rising aspect, with this sometimes causing conflict to it being a common symbol used by nobility of Rake descent.

Zenetta is typically depicted as a tall woman of athletic build with tanned bronze skin with several scars, patches of golden scales, fiery sun-like eyes, a pair of curved draconic horns abover her forehead arching back over her head and then up, and wavy flowing dark red or gold hair. In paintings and statues she normally wears tighter fitting clothing that is somewhat minimalistic in design, and somtimes with her having large gold dragon wings. She is the goddess of light, hope, and warmth in all aspects. Her pride and eagerness to achieve whatever she sets her mind to is a core aspect of her identity as a god. From the painting of the sky every sunrise, to mark the beginning of a new day, all the way to her restlessly chasing her love into the dark unknown of night. It is her light that guides all into a brighter future and out of the darkness of the past.

Prime Domains: Family, Soul, Sun

The mark of Rising Zenetta is usually drawn as a dragon sat atop a mount of gold with it's wings out and head held high, or simply as the upper half of a semi circle with three dominant rays pointing, two to the sides and slightly up and one directly up.
Domains: Ambition, Fire

The mark of Zenith Zenetta is usually drawn as a dragon in flight with its wings out and a short pointed ray above and below it, or simply as a cirlce with four dominant rays in the cardinal directions, but with the left and right rays being much longer.

Setting

The mark of Setting Zenetta is usually drawn as a curled up dragon sleeping atop a pile of gleaming gold, or simply as a full circle with three dominant rays, two to the sides and slightly down and one directly down.
Domains: Wealth, Toil

Eclipsed

The mark of Eclipsed Zenetta is usually drawn as the simplified form of Zenith Zenetta, but with the prominants rays shortand, and a darknned inner circle.
Domains: Darkness, Destruction

Orophet

The moon goddess, also known as the sly elf, is only slightly less commonly followed than her lover across Fluia. Eternally she dances and runs from Zenetta, hiding behind their children to tease the other and stoke the other's flames. Though what is most commonly meant in jest and desire, may some times turn more sinister. For this reason, be it from laughter or sadness, it is believed the stars she paints across the sky at night are in fact her own tears. Yet even despite her non-stop game with the sun goddess, she can still be seen at times slinking into the domain of brighter half to ensure that the lustful dragon still knows her sly elf holds her close. Thus it may be common to see her symbols hung from taverns, brothels, and the homes of newly weds of just about any culture. Those that follow her teachings and perfom magic though are often looked upon with suspiscion, due to the magics associated with her often being far more chaotic and formed through simple desire for they outcome rather than understanding of how it may be reached.

Orphette is typically depicted as a dainty and eerily beautiful elven woman with almost paper white skin, silvery hair, and deep sapphire eyes. In paintings and statues she is normally wearing long flowing robes that tend to be reflective, or black and silver. She is the goddess of love, imagination, and the ocean. Just as Zenetta paints the sky gorgeous shades orange and purple at dawn and dusk, Orophet spackles the nights with shimmering stars. It is in her wild creativity that many find their first steps into learning most find their first steps into learning magic before turning to the teachings of Agnosti to avoid falling into chaos. On the other hand, for sailors she is a life time compatriot and guide, with the bending of tides being at her will and it being by her tears that navigators find their way across the vast oceans.

Anador & Rodana

The twin nature god, also known as the landstriders, is one of the few gods you will almost never find a temple for of any kind, but who has shrines all across the lands. For druids, hunters, adventurers, and just about any known to wander far and wide, depictions of The Landstriders' symbol are often a welcome sight. One of Their core tenets is that no form of life can truly survive entirely on it's own, and thus to give to others just as you take from the wilds. Though if seen carved into the side of a cave rather than into the door of a cottage, it may be best to turn back before you find yourself facing nature's wrath. As while the giants that Anador and Ronada are often associated with are known to be peaceful beings, the monsterous races and beast that have saught shelter beneath them are rarely as welcoming.

Anador is typically depicted as a tall well built healthy young human-like man with lightly tanned skin, shoulder length oak brown hair, and emerald green eyes. Rodana is typically depicted as a short and sometimes sickly young human-like woman with ashen skin, waist length dark brown and grey hair, and earthy brown-green eyes. In paintings and statues both are normally wearing earthy or plant based coloured attire, but with Anador normally being dressed for spring or summer while Rodana appears dressed more for fall or winter with a snake resting on her shoulders.They are the god of the natural order, life, death, and travelers. Like two sides of a coin, one simply cannot be without the other, and thus in spite of being often seen as two figures, they are still only one god. It is from their harmony that adventurers and travelers are tought to keep in mind that where Anador lead, Rodana follows, and thus where there is life there will always be death, and it is not necessarily a bad thing.

Prime Domains: Nature, Travel

Okarine

The Sky god, also known as the Lightning of the North, is most commonly prayed to by those looking for a change in their life, be it large or small, or for guidence through a change they have little control over. Her temples tend to offer aid to those undergoing some form of transition period in their life and are seeking aid in either understanding or support. There are many legends about how while the sun godess was the leader of the six, it was the sky godess that encouraged them all. Thus while she is associated with change, she is also frequently depicted alongside Agnosti on the matter of changing into being a truer version of one's self. Though because of this, and some spoken legends of old, she has also come to be seen as a patron godess of transgender individuals.

Okarine is typically depicted as a short energetic woman with a lithe build and small chest, pale skin that has patches of silver-blue scales, bright blue eyes that seem to sparkle, and extremely long pale blue hair that flows behind her. In paintings and statues what she is wearing tends to depend on her aspect, with her wearing almost no clothes or very little transparent attire for clear, more covering attire for cloudy, and armour sparking with electricity for stormy. Depending on the artist, for clear she is also known to be drawn as a trans-fem as a showing of her her representation of change. She is known ot often spend time with both the sun and moon gods, usually hapilly offering the sky as a canvas for each to paint across.

Agnosti

The god of knowledge and magic, Also known as the Hidden One, is believed to have been the one to usher Fluia out of the dark era with bringing into existence the first written language since the schism. All across the lands, many hidden temples exist holding what is alleged to be their scriptures, writing solidified nature of the world into existence. Rather than texts on what was, recording the events of the dark era, Agnosti's texts instead describe what is and what shall be. Thus any time a temple to the Hidden One is found, it commonly becomes a site of greate interest to scholars, especially due to how criptic anything that has been translated has been. However, while these texts have helped shed new light in advancing the study and craft of magic, it is also believed that Agnosti and Orphet were often at odds in how they viewed magic should exist. While the moon godess felt it should be an artistic practice, performed much like how the gods are believed to use it, the knowledge god felt it should be a strict system with defined rules for what mortals could use to avoid another schism. Thus it has existed somewhere in between both ever since.

Agnosti is one of the only gods that is almost never depicted the same between two artists, with there existing know known details as to how they looked or even hint of their gender. Instead in paintings and statues they are most commonly depicted as a cloaked figure with gloves and a hood pulled to hide their features, a mask covering their visage, or an open book in front of their face. Alternatively they are shown from behind or facing just enough away from the viewer to make it impossible to discern just about any of their features. The reason for this exists written within their first texts, stating that the truest form of a god should never be known by mortals, and to show anything less would simply be a lie, thus to let whatever image is chosen to represent them instead represent all scholars and collected knowledge. However, it has been recorded that they have been known to speak to some of their followers, always offering guidence with the voice of someone that person holds as a font of knowledge and widsom.

Other Beings

Beyond the scope of the six, as described at the top, there do exist other god like beings. In many regions, while worship or the six is held in high regard, they may choose instead to follow a more local minor god that is specific to their village or people. There do also exist minor divine beings like fiends and celestials that will offer their aid more directly than the six in exchange for the person they are helping pledging themself to the being. Then beyond even that are what as known as the outsiders. Beings that do not hold any true position of control over any of the pillars of reality, yet still have a cult following for their god like power. Most outsiders are seen by the majority of people as corrupting beings born from the chaos of the schism, seeking only to finish what had been started. So while they may have worshippers, it is often not by the free will of those offering them homage.
